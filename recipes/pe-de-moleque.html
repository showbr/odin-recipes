Pé de moleque

Description:

Pé de Moleque peanut candy combines caramelized sugar with roasted peanuts for a rich, nutty, and perfectly crunchy, nostalgic Brazilian treat.

Ingredients:

  • Granulated sugar
  • Water
  • Roasted peanuts (unsalted and skinless)
  • Butter
  • Sweetened condensed milk (optional, for a softer texture)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Baking soda

Step by step

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread the peanuts in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for about 5-7 minutes, or until they're lightly golden and fragrant. This brings out the natural oils and enhances the nutty flavor. Let them cool before using.
  2. Meanwhile, grease a baking sheet or flat surface with butter and set it aside—this is where the candy will set, so make sure it's ready.
  3. In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the sugar and water over medium-high heat. Stir occasionally until the sugar completely dissolves, and the mixture starts to bubble gently (around 3-5 minutes).
  4. Let the sugar mixture continue to simmer without stirring until it turns a warm golden color (the right temperature is between 320-330°F with a candy thermometer). Keep an eye on it here, the sugar turns quickly!
  5. When your caramel reaches a golden color, add the butter and condensed milk (if using) and stir until combined. Don’t worry if it bubbles up a bit—that's normal! Quickly remove from heat and immediately stir in the baking soda. It'll foam up, giving the candy a light, airy texture.
  6. While the caramel is still foamy, quickly add the roasted peanuts and a dash of salt. Stir immediately to coat the peanuts.
  7. While still hot, pour the mixture onto the prepared baking sheet. Use a greased spatula to spread it evenly before it sets. For a twist sprinkle some fresh sea salt over the top. Allow the Pé de Moleque to cool for about 15 minutes.
  8. Before your pe de moleque fully cooled, flip it onto a greased cutting board. Slice it into squares or rectangles with a sharp knife, or break it apart into bite-sized pieces. Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week
